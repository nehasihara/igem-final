VitAC Yeast

Challenges Faced by ISRO and the Gaganyaan Mission

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is at the forefront of space exploration, with ambitious projects like the Gaganyaan mission aiming to send astronauts into space. However, ensuring the nutritional needs of astronauts is a critical concern for ISRO. Traditional space-proof food solutions often fall short in providing adequate levels of essential vitamins, posing a challenge for sustained human space exploration.

Consequences of Vitamin Deficiency in Space

Astronauts embarking on long-duration space missions face numerous challenges, including the prolonged deficiency of essential vitamins such as vitamin A and vitamin C. Vitamin deficiencies can lead to weakened immune systems, impaired vision, and decreased energy levels, posing significant risks to the health and performance of astronauts during their missions.

Introducing VitAC Yeast: A Space-Proof Solution

In response to the pressing need for space-proof nutritional solutions, iGEM IIT Roorkee presents VitAC Yeast, a groundbreaking project revolutionizing space nutrition. VitAC Yeast introduces genetically engineered yeast cells capable of synthesizing vitamin A and vitamin C, addressing the deficiencies faced by astronauts during extended space missions.

How VitAC Yeast Works ???

The genetically modified yeast can be integrated into baked food items such as bread, creating space-friendly nutritional supplements rich in essential vitamins. This innovative solution ensures that astronauts have access to the vital nutrients they need to maintain optimal health and performance during their missions.
